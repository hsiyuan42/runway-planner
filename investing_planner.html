Why compound growth matters more than the amount

At £100/mo for 10 years at 9.7%/yr, you contribute £12,000 but end up with roughly £20,000 — that's £8,000 in pure investment gain. At 20 years, contributions of £24,000 grow to ~£72,000. Time in the market, not the monthly amount, is the biggest variable. Starting now with £100 beats starting in 2 years with £200.

How to identify "money I genuinely won't need"

The question isn't really about your total savings — it's about layering your money so each pot has a clear purpose, risk level, and appropriate home. Only the bottom layer belongs in your S&P / PIE / Gold portfolio.

Your four money layers
🔴 Layer 1 — Emergency buffer · ~£3,500 · DO NOT invest
3 months of fixed costs (£790 × 3 = £2,370, rounded up). Sits in an easy-access savings account — Marcus or Chase UK currently pay ~4.5% AER. This money is entirely off-limits. It covers the worst case: job search stalls and income stops at exactly the same time. Without this, a bad month forces you to sell investments at potentially the worst moment.
🟡 Layer 2 — Runway reserve · ~£5,500 · DO NOT invest
Your active job-search buffer — covers 6–9 months of burn after June 2026 (when part-time income stops). Keep in cash or a short notice account. It must be accessible within weeks, not locked into markets that could be down 30% exactly when you need to withdraw. This is the layer that lets you focus fully on finding the right role without financial panic.
🟢 Layer 3 — Genuinely investable · ~£2,500 lump sum + £100/mo ongoing
What remains after Layers 1 & 2. Money you can leave completely untouched for 5+ years. This is where your S&P 500 / High Growth PIE / Gold allocation lives. Open a Stocks & Shares ISA — any gains inside are 100% tax-free up to £20k/yr. Best platforms for your allocation: Vanguard (lowest fees for S&P 500), Trading 212 (PIE investing, zero commission, fractional shares).
🟣 Layer 4 — Company equity · £4,931 est. · Treat as a windfall
Don't plan around it. If it materialises, it becomes a lump-sum addition to Layer 3 — or a one-off runway extension. Keeping it out of your monthly calculations avoids false confidence and ensures your other decisions remain grounded in what you can actually access today.

Practical answers

Is £100/mo right? Yes — well-calibrated for your situation. At current burn, it costs roughly 2 days of runway per month. The consistency of the habit matters far more than optimising between £50 and £150 right now.

Where to open? A Stocks & Shares ISA is the single most important structural decision. Gains are entirely tax-free within the £20k annual allowance. Vanguard is best for low-cost S&P 500 index exposure. Trading 212 supports PIE (Pie Investing Experience) with zero commission and fractional shares — suits your allocation well.

Watch-out on existing savings: Your crypto and non-cash investments (part of the £11,643) may already be in Layer 3 territory. If so, you could already have £2,000–2,500 invested — in which case, the £100/mo is just building on an existing position, which is exactly the right approach.
